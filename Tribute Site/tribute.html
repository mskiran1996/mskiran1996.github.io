Sir Jagadish Chandra Bose

The man who proved that plants also have the ability to feel pain and affection

Sir Jagadish Chandra Bose lecturing on the "nervous system" of plants at the Sorbonne in Paris in 1926

Here's a time line of Sir Jagadish Chandra Bose life:

  • 1858 - Born in Munshiganj, Bengal Presidency, (present-day Bangladesh) on 30 November.
  • 1869 - Bose did his schooling at Hare School and then St. Xavier's School at Kolkata.
  • 1875 - He passed the Entrance Examination (equivalent to school graduation) of the University of Calcutta and was admitted to St. Xavier's College, Calcutta.
  • 1879 - At St. Xavier's, Bose came in contact with Jesuit Father Eugene Lafont, who played a significant role in developing his interest in natural sciences.He received a BA from the University of Calcutta.
  • 1884 - He wanted to go to England to study for the Indian Civil Service. However, he changed his plans and decided to study medicine. This plan too did not suit him and once again he had to consider another option.Finally, he decided to study Natural Science and secured admission in Christ’s College, Cambridge. He completed his Natural Science Tripos from the college and pursued a BSc from the University of London earning his degree
  • 1885 - On his return to India,he was appointed as an officiating professor of physics in Presidency College on the request of Lord Ripon to the Director of Public Instruction.After some time the Director of Public Instruction and the Principal of the Presidency College made him permanent and paid him his full salary for the previous three years. Such was the character of J.C. Bose.There were many other issues in the college as well. The college did not had a proper laboratory and was not conducive for original research. Bose actually funded his research with his own money.
  • 1887 - He married Abala, the daughter of the renowned Brahmo reformer Durga Mohan Das, in 1887. She was a renowned feminist in her own right and fully supported her husband throughout his busy scientific career.
  • 1894 - Starting he experimented on the Hertzion waves in India and created the shortest radio-waves of 5mm.
  • 1895 - He conducted the first communication experiments thereby becoming the pioneer in multimedia communication.He presented his first scientific paper, ‘On the polarization of Electric Rays by Double Reflecting Crystals’ before the Asiatic Society of Bengal in May.
  • 1896 - His papers were later published by the Royal Society of London.He met Marconi who was also working on wireless signaling experiment.He was also a writer and authored ‘Niruddesher Kahini’ which was the first major work in Bengali science fiction. This story was later translated into English.
  • 1899 - He developed the “iron-mercury-iron coherer with telephone detector” which he presented at the Royal Society.He was also a pioneer in the field of biophysics and was the first one to suggest that plants too can feel pain and understand affection.
  • 1903 - He was made Companion of the Order of the Indian Empire.
  • 1912 - He was also made Companion of the Order of the Star of India in recognition of his contributions to science.
  • 1937 - He died at the age of 78 on 23 November at Giridih, Bengal Presidency, British India (now Giridih, Jharkhand, India)
